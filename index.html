Managing the portfolio
Process beats prediction. These keep an equal-weight portfolio disciplined.
01

Hold the weights

Each position is sized at 10% on purpose. When a winner grows past roughly 13 to 15%, trim it back toward 10% and redistribute to the laggards.

02

Rebalance on a schedule

Pick a cadence and stick to it. Monthly matches the model. Only act off-schedule if a position breaks through your rebalance band.

03

Keep cash in reserve

Being fully invested leaves nothing to deploy when the market sells off. A small reserve lets you add at lower prices instead of just watching.

04

Define your exit first

Decide what would make you sell each position before you buy it. Sell on a broken thesis, not a single red day, so you are not improvising in a drawdown.

05

Let it compound

The edge is in repeating the process, not in any single trade. Once the screener assigns the ten, give it time and resist the urge to tinker.

06

Feed the screener

The portfolio is only as good as the names entering it. Each month the model re-ranks and refreshes the ten so the weights size themselves.
